Tom Brady (Thomas Edward Patrick Brady Jr., born August 3, 1977, in San Mateo, California) is a retired American football quarterback whose fashion and advertising career — spanning from a two-and-a-half-year contract as the face of Stetson cologne in 2007 to a runway debut for Gucci in Times Square in May 2026 — constitutes one of the most extensive and commercially significant bodies of campaign work by any athlete in the history of men’s fashion advertising. He replaced Matthew McConaughey as the Stetson man, grew UGG’s men’s business by 150% over six years as its brand ambassador, became the face of TAG Heuer, Under Armour, and IWC Schaffhausen, co-founded his own apparel brand, and then walked the Gucci catwalk in all-black leather alongside Paris Hilton, Emily Ratajkowski, and Cindy Crawford. He was married to Gisele Bündchen, the highest-paid supermodel in the world, from 2009 to 2022.[1][2]

Early life

Brady was born in San Mateo, California, the only son and youngest of four children of Tom Brady Sr., an insurance executive, and Galynn Patricia Brady. He grew up worshiping Joe Montana, attended Junipero Serra High School, and was drafted 199th overall by the New England Patriots in the 2000 NFL Draft out of the University of Michigan — a draft position so low it became, over the next two decades, the single most famous example of talent evaluation failure in the history of professional sports.[1]

Fragrance

Brady’s relationship with fragrance is more personal and more commercially significant than any other male athlete’s. Three fragrances define the arc.

Stetson Original — The first cologne Brady ever wore. He found it in his father’s medicine cabinet as a teenager in San Mateo. A chypre fragrance launched in 1981 by Coty, with notes of citrus, patchouli, amber, musk, vanilla, and tonka bean, Stetson Original was the number-one mass-market men’s fragrance in America when Brady signed on as its face in April 2007. The two-and-a-half-year contract made him the official Stetson spokesperson, replacing Matthew McConaughey. The campaign, dubbed “Set Free,” was shot at Big Sky Ranch in Simi Valley, California, by Laird and Partners. The TV spot featured Brady driving a vintage Mustang convertible alongside a galloping thoroughbred racehorse, scored to Foghat’s “Slow Ride.” He was the first Stetson model in the brand’s history not to wear the iconic cowboy hat. “I roll around in the dirt for a living,” he said on set. Coty CEO Bernd Beetz called him “the ideal male icon for the Stetson brand.” The print campaign ran nationwide and the TV spot aired during NFL programming for three years.[3][4][5]

Stetson All American — In 2009, Brady fronted the campaign for Stetson’s new flanker fragrance, All American, a spicy aromatic created by master perfumer Harry Fremont at Firmenich. The ads had him kicking gravel in a dirtied white long-sleeve tee and posing on a dirt bike. Stetson described him as “confident, rugged, masculine — a strong, masculine risk-taker with undeniable sex appeal.” The fragrance features top notes of ginger and guava, heart notes of nutmeg, sage, suede, and fern, and base notes of amber, patchouli, musk, and vetiver.[6]

Tom Ford Tobacco Vanille — Brady’s personal fragrance of choice. In an interview with Bloomberg, he named Tom Ford as his favorite designer: “I like Tom Ford a lot because it’s so classic and has great quality.” Tobacco Vanille — a warm, sweet, tobacco-forward Private Blend fragrance launched in 2007 — became associated with Brady through fragrance community reporting and his known preference for Tom Ford across both suiting and scent. The connection between Brady and Tom Ford suits is well documented; the fragrance link follows the same aesthetic line. At $300 for 50ml, it occupies a different commercial universe than the drugstore Stetson he grew up wearing — and the distance between those two bottles tells the story of Brady’s entire trajectory.[7][8]

Fashion and campaign work

Brady’s fashion campaign work is not a sideline to his football career. It is a parallel career that began in 2007 and has, since his retirement in 2023, become his primary visual identity.

Under Armour (2010–present) — Brady was the first NFL quarterback to sign an endorsement deal with Under Armour. The partnership has lasted over fifteen years and survived his launch of a competing apparel brand, his move from New England to Tampa Bay, and his retirement. He appeared in Under Armour marketing for more than a decade, wear-testing proprietary muscle-recovery technology. “I’m basically a 38-year-old guinea pig,” he said in 2015.[9][10]

UGG Australia (2011–2016) — Brady became brand ambassador for UGG For Men in 2011. Over six years, UGG’s men’s business grew by more than 150%. In 2015, a Facebook photo of his UGG Grantt slippers propped up in front of a fire garnered 211,000 likes and 11,000 shares, becoming the visual seed for the brand’s “Do Nothing” campaign. The 2016 fall campaign paired Brady with actor Jeff Bridges and teammate Julian Edelman in a five-part digital series celebrating “a man’s right to Do Nothing.” The campaign was produced by Camp + King and ran across digital and print channels. UGG described Brady as someone who “knows firsthand the importance of sometimes taking time out to do nothing.”[11][12]

TAG Heuer (2015) — Brady became brand ambassador for TAG Heuer at a New York launch event on October 13, 2015, introducing the Carrera Heuer 01, a $5,450 timepiece. He appeared alongside TAG Heuer CEO Jean-Claude Biver. “The first nice watch I purchased was a TAG,” Brady said. “So, it looks like I’ve now come full circle.”[13]

IWC Schaffhausen (2019) — Brady signed with the Swiss luxury watchmaker owned by Richemont in the summer of 2019, adding a second prestige watch partnership to his portfolio.[14]

Movado — An earlier watch endorsement, prior to the TAG Heuer and IWC deals.[15]

Aston Martin — Brady became an ambassador for the British luxury carmaker, aligning himself with the same visual language of British craftsmanship and understated performance that defined his later brand partnerships.[15]

Earlier endorsements include The Gap, Dunkin’ Donuts, Wheaties, Glaceau Smartwater, Simmons Beautyrest, Nike, Intel, Visa, and Foot Locker.[15]

Gucci

On Saturday, May 16, 2026, Tom Brady made his runway debut at the GucciCore Cruise 2027 fashion show in Times Square, New York City. Gucci shut down Broadway between 46th and 48th Streets and turned every digital billboard in Times Square into a live stream of the show. Brady, 48, walked the runway in an all-black leather jacket, matching leather pants, and dress shoes, accessorized with a silver watch. He gave the cameras a cool smirk as he made his way down the catwalk.[16][17]

He was hand-selected by creative director Demna, who was staging his first Cruise collection for the Italian fashion house. The runway was inspired by artist Robert Longo’s iconic “Men in the Cities” series. Other models included Paris Hilton, Emily Ratajkowski, Candice Swanepoel, and Cindy Crawford, who closed the show. The front row included Mariah Carey, Shawn Mendes, Lindsay Lohan, Kim Kardashian, and Anna Wintour. Social media compared his stride to “GOAT status” and “Terminator vibes.” Former teammates Rob Gronkowski and Julian Edelman responded with three goat emojis on their podcast.[16][17][18]

BRADY brand

In January 2022, Brady launched BRADY, a technical apparel brand co-founded with Jens Grede (co-founder of Skims and Frame) and designer Dao-Yi Chow (co-founder of Public School, two-time CFDA Award winner). The brand launched exclusively at Nordstrom after more than three years in development, with 45 initial styles ranging from $20 to $495. Brady served as the primary model for the brand’s “Train” and “Live” collections and cast elite NCAA college athletes alongside himself, including Shedeur Sanders. In January 2024, BRADY and TB12 merged with sportswear company NOBULL, making Brady the second-largest shareholder behind co-founder Mike Repole.[19][20][21]

Football career

Brady was drafted 199th overall in the 2000 NFL Draft and went on to become the most decorated player in the history of professional football: seven Super Bowl championships (XXXVI, XXXVIII, XXXIX, XLIX, LI, LIII, LV), five Super Bowl MVP awards, three NFL MVP awards, 15 Pro Bowl selections, and every major passing record in the sport. He played twenty seasons for the New England Patriots (2000–2019) and three for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers (2020–2022), retiring in February 2023 at age 45. He is universally referred to as the greatest football player of all time.[1]

Legacy

Tom Brady’s fashion career is not a post-retirement hobby. It began in 2007, when he was thirty years old, already dating the world’s highest-paid supermodel, and approaching his first Stetson shoot with the observation that he “rolls around in the dirt for a living.” It continued through six years growing UGG’s men’s business by 150%, through fifteen years with Under Armour, through luxury watch partnerships with TAG Heuer and IWC, through founding his own apparel brand with the same team behind Skims, and through a Gucci runway debut at forty-eight in Times Square, hand-selected by Demna, walking alongside Cindy Crawford.

The fragrance arc alone tells the story. He found Stetson Original in his father’s medicine cabinet in San Mateo. He became its campaign face twenty years later, replacing Matthew McConaughey. He fronted Stetson All American — the dirtbike, the gravel, the white tee. And then, somewhere along the way, he started wearing Tom Ford Tobacco Vanille at $300 a bottle — the same designer whose suits he wore to every postgame press conference for two decades. The distance between the drugstore Stetson and the Tom Ford Private Blend is the distance between the 199th pick and seven Super Bowls. Brady said at the 2026 FIFA World Cup, that he also wears a new cologne called Morph by House of Salisbury.
