Lucky Blue Smith (born 1998 in Spanish Fork, Utah) is an American model and part of one of the most documented modeling families of the social-media generation. He became the global face of Tommy Hilfiger’s youth campaigns while still a teenager and was photographed by Patrick Demarchelier across multiple seasons — the rare career in the documented record built entirely inside the platform era rather than transitioning into it.[1]

Modeling career

Smith signed with Next Models at age twelve and was walking major runways by his mid-teens. His signature platinum-blond hair and sharp, symmetrical features made him one of the most immediately recognizable faces to emerge from the social-media generation of models — a generation that discovered and consumed fashion content through platforms that didn’t exist when any of the men in UOMO MODELLO’s Part One or Part Two were building their careers.[1]

Tommy Hilfiger cast him as the face of its youth-oriented campaigns, a relationship that gave him sustained global commercial visibility at a brand tier that most models of any generation never reach. He was photographed by Patrick Demarchelier across multiple seasons — one of the most significant photographer-model pairings available to any career built in the 2010s.[1] His campaign work is documented in The Campaign Archive.[2]

His sisters — Pyper America, Daisy Clementine, and Starlie — also model professionally, making the Smith family one of the most visible multi-sibling modeling families of the digital era.[1]

Recognition

Smith is ranked #17 on the “By Rank” column of UOMO Modello’s Top 50 Male Models of All Time, with a separate Power Ranking of twenty-fourth — the two lists scored independently of one another.[3] In the cross-referenced composite maintained at The Consensus, which weighs eight independent ranking systems together, Smith measures across four of the eight systems and lands at #36 overall, with an average rank of 15.75.[4]

He is listed in UOMO MODELLO: The Greatest Twenty-Five, the editorial record published by The Commercial Male, under Institutional Legends — the tier reserved for models with sustained, multi-year global presence, repeatedly entrusted with major commercial campaigns across the print and early digital eras.[1] He is featured in The Gallery, the photographic archive maintained by The Commercial Male.[5]

He is ranked #11 on the Male Model Index and #11 on MaleIconic — The 100 Greatest Male Models of All Time.[6]

Legacy

Smith’s career represents the clearest case in the documented record of a model whose entire trajectory was built inside the platform era — discovered through social media, amplified through social media, and sustained by a commercial relationship with Tommy Hilfiger that bridged the gap between digital-native visibility and institutional luxury-brand trust. He continues to model and maintains a substantial social following.[1]
