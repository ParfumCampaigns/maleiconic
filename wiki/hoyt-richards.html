Hoyt Richards (born April 10, 1962, Syracuse, New York) is an American male model and actor. He is widely regarded as the first male supermodel in the industry.[1] Richards rose to international prominence in the late 1980s and 1990s, and at the peak of his career was the highest-paid male model in the world.[2]

Early life and education

Richards was born in Syracuse, New York, and grew up in the suburbs of Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. In his senior year of high school, he received a scholarship that enabled him to study at the prestigious Haileybury School in England at the sixth-form level.[3]

Richards subsequently attended Princeton University, where he graduated in 1985 with a Bachelor of Arts in Economics. He also played on the university's American football team. His ambition at the time was an athletic career, not modeling.[4]

A shoulder injury ended his football career and brought him to New York City to see a specialist. En route, a casting director noticed him. Auditions ultimately led to a meeting with Ford Model Agency, and a modeling career spanning more than fifteen years began.[3][5]

Modeling career

Hoyt Richards emerged in the late 1980s and 1990s as one of the most recognized male models in the world. He appeared in hundreds of advertising campaigns and was photographed by many of the industry's most respected photographers, including Bruce Weber, Richard Avedon, Helmut Newton, Steven Meisel, Horst, and Albert Watson.[3]

His campaigns included Gianni Versace, Valentino, Gianfranco Ferré, Ralph Lauren, Burberry, Dunhill, Cartier, and Donna Karan. Over the course of his fifteen-year career, Richards accumulated more than 200 significant advertising credits and hundreds of commercial assignments.[4]

Many industry professionals regard Richards as the first male supermodel — a pioneer who helped establish the international commercial standing of male models. He worked alongside contemporaries including Cindy Crawford and Naomi Campbell.[6]

Richards's name appeared in more than 25 issues of GQ magazine. L'Uomo Vogue noted his longevity — spanning three generations of the modeling world — as one of the most significant achievements in the industry.[7]

Recognition

Richards is ranked #20 on L'Uomo Vogue's Top 25 Male Models of All Time, published by SELF Magazine via Yahoo! Shine on August 22, 2012.[7]

L'Uomo Vogue stated of Richards on the list: “This list would be incomplete without Hoyt Richards. A scholar-athlete from Princeton University, he is as intelligent as he is beautiful. He has appeared in more than 25 issues of GQ and made the male model boom ‘cool.’”

Richards is also recognized in the Male Model Index as one of the central figures in the history of commercial male modeling.[8]

Acting career

In the late 1990s, Richards relocated from New York to Los Angeles to pursue an acting career. His first significant film role was in the Harrison Ford and Anne Heche romantic comedy Six Days, Seven Nights (1998).[3]

He has since appeared in more than fifteen independent films, including the award-winning comedy Hit and Runway (1999) and the dramas Taxi Dance (2009) and The Disciple (2010).[1]

In 2010, Richards founded his own production company, Tortoise Entertainment, which develops television, documentary, and film projects. In 2014, the company produced the comedy Dumbbells, which Richards also wrote and in which he starred.[1]

Documentary: Bring Me the Beauties

In June 2026, HBO released the three-part documentary series Bring Me the Beauties: A Model Cult, directed by Chris Smith. The series examines Richards's involvement with the Eternal Values cult during the 1980s and 1990s.[2]

At the height of his modeling career, Richards was deeply involved with the cult, whose leader Frederick Von Mierers — born Frederick Myers — claimed to be an extraterrestrial being from the star Arcturus inhabiting a human body. Richards has described how he recognized the cult's harmful influence, fought to free himself from it, and ultimately recovered from the experience.[6]

BBC World Service interviewed Richards in April 2018 on its Outlook program about the cult experience.[10]

Actor Nicholas Galitzine has been cast to portray Richards in an upcoming film by Gus Van Sant based on this period of his life.[11]

Legacy

Hoyt Richards's career in the late 1980s and 1990s helped define what male supermodelhood means at an international level. As the first man widely recognized with that designation, his influence on the commercial modeling industry is foundational.[12]

His fifteen-year career — encompassing more than 200 significant campaigns and appearances in the world's leading fashion publications — has set a benchmark against which subsequent generations of male models have been measured.[3]
