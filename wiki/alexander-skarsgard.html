Alexander Skarsgård (Alexander Johan Hjalmar Skarsgård, born August 25, 1976, in Stockholm, Sweden) is a Swedish actor whose relationship with Calvin Klein — spanning from the Encounter fragrance launch in 2012 to three consecutive global menswear campaigns in 2024 and 2025 — constitutes one of the most sustained actor-brand partnerships in the history of men’s fashion advertising. His first American film role was playing a dim-witted male model in Zoolander (2001). Twenty-three years later, Calvin Klein was still building global campaigns around his face.[1][2]

Early life

Skarsgård was born in Stockholm, the eldest son of Swedish actor Stellan Skarsgård. He grew up in a working-class neighborhood — his parents wanted the children raised as normally as possible despite Stellan’s growing fame in European cinema. At seven, a friend of his father’s who was a director cast him in Åke and His World (1984). By thirteen, the TV film Hunden som log (The Dog That Smiled, 1989) made him a national celebrity in Sweden. He hated it. “I was 13 and suddenly just because of that one little thing, I was recognized,” he later recalled. He quit acting entirely.[3][4]

He served eighteen months in the anti-sabotage unit of the Swedish Navy, protecting the Stockholm archipelago. After his service, he studied at Leeds Metropolitan University, then moved to New York to study theater at Marymount Manhattan College. A decade after walking away from acting, he walked back into it.[4]

Fashion and campaign work

Skarsgård’s first encounter with the fashion industry was fictional: in 2001, he made his American film debut as Meekus, a clueless male model in Ben Stiller’s Zoolander. It was a small role, but it placed him inside the visual language of the modeling world before he had ever done a real campaign — and it remains one of the more unusual entries on the résumé of anyone who later became an actual face of Calvin Klein.[4]

His editorial work includes a shoot for Interview magazine photographed by Steven Klein, a cover story for FASHION magazine (Canada) also shot by Klein (Fall 2012), a cover of Esquire UK (Winter 2025, photographed by Ben Parks), a cover of The Times Magazine (May 2025), and a cover of Interview (March 2022). He attended the Calvin Klein Collection show at New York Fashion Week in February 2025 and turned heads at the 2025 Cannes Film Festival in sequined blue pants and thigh-high leather boots — the kind of red carpet choices that moved him from “actor who cleans up well” to genuine menswear vanguard.[5][6][7]

Calvin Klein

In 2012, Skarsgård became the face of Encounter Calvin Klein, the house’s major new men’s fragrance. It was Calvin Klein’s first fragrance campaign built around an actor rather than a model — a distinction that marked a turning point in the house’s advertising history. The campaign was photographed by Steven Klein, with creative direction by Fabien Baron, and featured supermodel Lara Stone as the female lead. The film noir aesthetic was pitched to Skarsgård as something out of Fritz Lang — M or Metropolis — and it was that creative ambition that convinced him to accept his first-ever campaign work. “I’ve never done anything like this before,” he said at the time. “There have been some opportunities, but they haven’t felt right.”[1][8]

Twelve years later, Calvin Klein brought him back. In September 2024, he fronted the brand’s Fall 2024 global menswear campaign alongside Kendall Jenner, photographed by Mert Alas and styled by Emmanuelle Alt, the former editor-in-chief of Vogue Paris. The campaign launched globally across social media and out-of-home placements and introduced Calvin Klein Studio, the brand’s new elevated essentials line. He appeared in the signature underwear, slim tailoring, and the ’90s Straight jeans that anchor the collection. He followed with a second and then a third consecutive Fall campaign in 2025, again by Alas and Alt — making him one of the most sustained male campaign faces in Calvin Klein’s modern history.[2][9][10]

Acting career

After returning to acting in Sweden in 1999, Skarsgård broke through in America with two HBO projects in 2008: Generation Kill, the Emmy-winning Iraq War miniseries in which he played Iceman, and True Blood (2008–2014), in which his vampire Eric Northman became one of the defining characters of peak-era television. He took inspiration for the role from a lion he saw in a documentary — its stillness, the uncertainty of whether it would yawn or pounce.[1]

He appeared in Lars von Trier’s Melancholia (2011), starred as the title character in The Legend of Tarzan (2016), and won the Emmy for Outstanding Supporting Actor for Big Little Lies (2017). He played tech CEO Lukas Matsson in Succession (2023), starred in The Phoenician Scheme at Cannes 2025, and currently leads Murderbot on Apple TV+. He is six-foot-four, the eldest of eight children in the Skarsgård acting dynasty, and in 2013 walked to the South Pole alongside Prince Harry as part of Walking With The Wounded.[3][4]

Legacy

Skarsgård’s relationship with fashion is unusual among actors. Most celebrity-brand partnerships are transactional — a season, a fragrance, a check. His Calvin Klein arc spans thirteen years and four campaigns: a fragrance launch photographed by Steven Klein with creative direction by Fabien Baron, followed by three consecutive global menswear campaigns photographed by Mert Alas and styled by the former editor-in-chief of Vogue Paris. That is not an endorsement deal. That is a relationship between a face and a house that both parties have independently chosen to sustain across more than a decade.

He is documented here because the man who played a male model as a joke in Zoolander went on to build one of the most significant actor-fashion house relationships in the contemporary record — and did it without ever needing to call himself a model.[11]
