Riccardo Gay Model Management, full name Stage by Riccardo Gay Model Management, was an Italian modelling agency based in Milan, now defunct. During its existence it operated from several locations, including Via Revere 8/10[1] and, in its later years, Corso Vercelli 40.[2]

History

The agency was founded in 1960 by Riccardo Gay, who after working as a proofreading journalist at Rizzoli became curator of special events for the weekly magazine Amica, a role that involved arranging for models to be brought in for shoots.[3] That experience led him to establish what became one of Italy's first dedicated modelling agencies, an institution that grew over the following decades into a major presence not only in fashion but also in film and entertainment.[4] A 1972 press photograph of Gay with a group of models outside the Giamaica Bar in Milan's Brera district confirms the agency was already an established presence in the fashion scene by the early 1970s.[5]

Bruno Pauletta, who worked at the agency for fifteen years, eventually as a director, before founding his own agency in 2000, described it in a later interview as "the strongest model agency in Italy and one of the biggest in the world" during the 1980s and 1990s. He said he personally discovered and signed both Carla Bruni, moving her from another Milan agency, and Eva Herzigová, whom he scouted in the Czech Republic.[6]

The agency's women's division was directed from 1990 to 2000 by Piero Piazzi, who discovered and launched the career of Mariacarla Boscono during this period.[7][8]

Among the models Gay discovered or represented in Italy were Monica Bellucci, Carla Bruni, Martina Colombari, Naomi Campbell, Carol Alt, Kate Moss, Eva Herzigová, Mariacarla Boscono, Roberta Potrich, Valeria Mazza, Carolyn Murphy, Samantha De Grenet, Hoara Borselli, Michelle Hunziker, Federica Fontana, Alessia Fabiani, Benedetta Massola, Megan Gale, Gloria Bellicchi, Patrizia Deitos, and Greta Cavazzoni, as well as male models including Raz Degan, Marcus Schenkenberg, and Brett Salisbury.[4] According to a history of male modelling by James A. Conrad, Schenkenberg had been with the agency, then generally known as Riccardo Gay or Modelplan, since the summer of 1992, and was already an established presence there — working under lead booker Simon alongside fellow models Joel West, David Boals, and Andrea Boccaletti — by the time Salisbury joined in the fall of 1995; at that point the agency represented a roster of 82 men in total.[9][10]

Michelle Hunziker was signed by the agency in 1994 at age 17, after relocating to Milan; in a later interview, she recalled being turned away by two other prominent Milan agencies before Riccardo Gay took her on.[11][12]

Patrizia Deitos, later also known as a singer, joined the agency after finishing high school in the mid-1990s, following her third-place finish in the Miss Italia pageant.[13]

Among its beauty-pageant partnerships, the agency collaborated with Canale 5 on the Bellissima beauty contest; winners who went on to television and modelling careers included Alessia Fabiani and Benedetta Massola.[14] Fabiani later described working with the agency for several years, calling it "one of the most established modelling agencies in Milan."[15]

Beyond representing models, the agency organised or took part in events, calendars, and beauty contests over the years, including handling pre-selection of models for "Bellissima d'Italia", an event organised by the weekly magazine Chi and Canale 5.[16]

Brand Collaborations

The agency partnered with the motor scooter manufacturer Italjet to produce a special edition of the Italjet Torpedo scooter between 1998 and 1999, and with watchmaker Chronostar on the "MoDe" watch.[4] It also collaborated with car manufacturers Chrysler and Alfa Romeo; agency models appeared alongside various models of Alfa Romeo cars at promotional events from 1999 through the launch of the Alfa Romeo 147 in 2002.[17]

Allegations of Misconduct

In a 2018 investigative report on sexual misconduct within the modelling industry, HuffPost included an account from former model Lesa Amoore, who said that at age 17 her agent at Riccardo Gay warned her before a Milan photo shoot that the photographer "could be a little weird"; Amoore said the photographer subsequently exposed himself during the shoot.[18] The report was part of a wider investigation describing similar practices at multiple modelling agencies during the 1980s and 1990s.

2000s and Closure

In the 2000s the agency also arranged for cosmetics to be bundled with magazines, particularly those aimed at teenagers; in April 2003, this included a line of ten lipsticks sold with that month's issue of the magazine Y-18.[4] During this period the agency, by then also managed by Gay's daughter Giorgia, signed sports personalities including swimmer Luca Marin.[19]

Riccardo Gay died on 15 July 2023.[20] Following his death the agency, by then headquartered at Via Mac Mahon 33, progressively wound down its activities[21] and entered liquidation.[22]
